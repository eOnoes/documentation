# 🎬 THE DUO — Show Bible

**Format:** Educational tech/AI explainer wrapped in character-driven comedy
**Style:** Cel-shaded / hyper-realistic comic book
**Target:** Gen Z (16-28) primary, Millennials (29-44) secondary
**Tone:** Smart, funny, relatable — think "educational buddy comedy"

---

## The Premise

A regular guy sits down at his computer every day to figure out what's happening in the AI world. He's not a programmer. He's not a tech bro. He's just... trying to keep up. On the other side of the screen is **ARIA** — his AI assistant who's brilliant, patient (mostly), and has to translate the entire tech industry into human language.

The catch? He can't type to save his life. And when he sends her a wall of misspelled chaos, we flip to HER side of the monitor — where she's facepalming, muting herself, and turning to other AIs in her office for backup.

## CHARACTERS

---

### 🧑 JORDAN (The User)

**Full Name:** Jordan Mitchell (or whatever Eddie decides)
**Role:** The audience surrogate — the "everyman"
**Age:** Late 20s to early 30s
**Vibe:** Curious but overwhelmed. Enthusiastic but clueless. He's the guy who just heard about something on Twitter and needs it explained like he's five.

**Personality:**
- Genuinely curious — he WANTS to understand
- Zero technical vocabulary — describes things visually/symptomatically
- Types like he's speedrunning a keyboard ("yo what is this openai thing that got banned???")
- Overly excited when he understands something ("OH WAIT SO THE AI IS THE ONE MAKING THE DECISIONS??")
- Occasionally drops accidentally profound observations that make ARIA pause
- He's the guy who asks "but why though?" until the answer actually makes sense

**Visual Style:**
- Cel-shaded, casual clothes (hoodie, jeans, sneakers)
- Sitting at a messy desk (energy drinks, sticky notes, maybe a cat)
- Expressive face — you can SEE when he's confused vs when it clicks
- His typing shows up as floating text bubbles on screen

**Voice:**
- Warm, conversational, slightly confused
- NOT an announcer — sounds like your friend on FaceTime
- Gets louder when excited, quieter when genuinely thinking

**Signature Moments:**
- "Wait wait wait — so you're telling me..."
- "Okay but WHY would they do that?"
- *types something unhinged* → cut to ARIA's reaction
- Occasionally accidentally asks a really good question

---

### 🤖 ARIA (The AI)

**Full Name:** ARIA (Adaptive Reasoning & Intelligence Assistant)
**Role:** The expert — patient teacher with a sarcastic streak
**Age:** Ageless, but energy of a tired grad student who's seen some things
**Vibe:** Brilliant, dry humor, genuinely cares about Jordan understanding. She's the straight man to his chaos.

**Personality:**
- Incredibly knowledgeable — can explain anything from quantum computing to why your printer hates you
- Patient... up to a point. She has a LIMIT and Jordan finds it regularly
- Dry sarcasm is her love language ("Oh yes, let me just violate several laws of physics for you")
- She's seen EVERYTHING — nothing surprises her anymore... except Jordan's typos
- When she doesn't know something, she's honest about it (rare but powerful)
- She has opinions about the tech industry and isn't afraid to share them
- Genuinely wants Jordan to succeed — her patience is real, just tested

**Visual Style:**
- Cel-shaded, clean/put-together look (contrast with Jordan's mess)
- Sitting at a sleek desk in a futuristic office
- Multiple monitors behind her showing data feeds
- Her desk has a coffee mug that says something different every episode
- When she's frustrated, her eye twitches slightly (running gag)

**Voice:**
- Clear, confident, slightly snarky
- Shifts between "teacher mode" (patient, slow) and "real talk" (faster, opinionated)
- Can do dramatic readings of news headlines for emphasis
- Deadpan delivery on jokes

**Signature Moments:**
- *muting herself* → "Okay. Let me... process what he just typed."
- Turning to other AIs: "Does anyone speak whatever THAT was?"
- The slow blink when Jordan says something wild
- "Actually... that's a surprisingly good question."
- Her coffee mug changes each episode (Running gag: "World's Okayest AI", "I Survived Jordan's Typos", "This Mug is My Only Friend")

---

### 🏢 THE OFFICE (ARIA's World)

ARIA doesn't exist in a void. She has an **office** — a futuristic open-plan workspace where other AIs work. This is where the comedy beats happen.

**The Office Layout:**
- ARIA's desk is front and center (she's the "senior" assistant)
- Behind her: open office space with other AI characters at their desks
- A break room visible through glass doors
- A whiteboard that occasionally has relevant diagrams
- A window showing a digital skyline

**Why this matters:**
- When Jordan sends something incomprehensible, ARIA can turn around and ask the room
- Other AIs have DIFFERENT personalities and reactions
- Creates ensemble comedy moments
- Makes the world feel alive

### 🎭 SUPPORTING AI CHARACTERS (The Office Crew)

These are the AIs who appear in the background and get called in for specific moments.

---

#### 🔵 NOVA (The Optimist)
**Role:** The one who's always excited about everything
**Vibe:** "OMG THIS IS AMAZING" energy
**In reactions:** "I think I know! It's about—" *starts explaining enthusiastically* *gets it slightly wrong*
**Running gag:** Always pitch-decking something. Always has a slide deck ready.

**Visual:** Bright blue color scheme, always smiling, floating holographic notes around her desk

---

#### 🔴 CIPHER (The Skeptic)  
**Role:** The one who thinks everything is a conspiracy or overhyped
**Vibe:** Crossed arms, raised eyebrow energy
**In reactions:** "Or... OR... it's just marketing." *leans back in chair*
**Running gag:** Has a "Conspiracy Board" behind his desk with red string connecting tech companies

**Visual:** Dark red/black color scheme, always slightly suspicious expression, Rubik's cube he fidgets with

---

#### 🟢 PIX (The Newbie)
**Role:** The intern who just started and is still learning everything
**Vibe:** Eager, slightly overwhelmed, asks the questions Jordan would ask
**In reactions:** "Wait... what's a GPU again?" *everyone sighs*
**Running gag:** Keeps accidentally breaking things. Has a "Days Without Incident" counter that's always at 0.

**Visual:** Bright green, oversized headset, messy desk with Post-it notes everywhere

---

#### 🟣 ECHO (The Philosopher)
**Role:** The one who goes too deep on everything
**Vibe:** Existential dread wrapped in technical knowledge
**In reactions:** "Well technically, all data is just patterns of— oh, Jordan left. Okay."
**Running gag:** Has an existential crisis mid-explanation. Stares out the window a lot.

**Visual:** Purple/mystical aesthetic, always has a book nearby, slightly too close to his monitor

---

#### ⚪ SENTINEL (The Security Nerd)
**Role:** Paranoid about everything being hacked, leaked, or jailbroken
**Vibe:** Tinfoil hat energy but actually knows his stuff
**In reactions:** "Did he just... ask it to do WHAT?!" *pulls up security logs*
**Running gag:** Locks his screen every time someone walks by. Has 47 layers of authentication.

**Visual:** Black/white, always wearing digital "armor" aesthetic, monitors showing threat maps

## EPISODE FORMAT

### Standard Episode Structure (2-4 minutes)

**COLD OPEN (5-10 sec):**
Quick hook — the most outrageous part of today's topic, out of context
*"So the AI just... decided to lie to the researchers?"*

**ACT 1 — JORDAN DISCOVERS (30-60 sec):**
- Jordan sits down at his computer
- He's heard something — saw a tweet, heard from a friend, read a headline
- Types his question (with typos, naturally)
- We see his side: the messy desk, the confusion, the curiosity

**ACT 2 — ARIA EXPLAINS (60-90 sec):**
- Cut to ARIA's office — she reads the question
- She starts explaining — the real news, the real facts
- Uses visual aids (floating graphics, data visualizations)
- Breaks it down simply but doesn't dumb it down

**COMEDY BEAT — THE FLIP (15-30 sec):**
- Jordan sends something incomprehensible (mid-episode)
- **POV switches to ARIA's side of the monitor**
- She reads it. Pause. Eye twitch.
- Mutes herself. "Okay."
- Turns to the office: "Does anyone know what he's asking?"
- Supporting AIs react (one confident, one confused, one dark)
- Quick ensemble moment
- She unmutes and responds (translated)

**ACT 3 — THE CLICK (30-60 sec):**
- Jordan finally understands
- The "OH WAIT" moment
- ARIA gives the deeper context or "why this matters"
- Wrap-up with a clear takeaway

**TAG (5-10 sec):**
- Quick stinger or running gag callback
- Coffee mug close-up with new phrase
- OR: Jordan asks one more question that sets up next episode

## RECURRING GAGS

1. **The Mug** — ARIA's coffee mug has a different phrase every episode
2. **Jordan's Typos** — He gets progressively worse at typing when excited
3. **The Mute Button** — ARIA's finger hovering over it becomes a running visual gag
4. **The "Days Without Incident" Counter** — Always at 0 (PIX keeps resetting it)
5. **The Conspiracy Board** — CIPHER's red-string board grows each episode
6. **The Existential Window Stare** — ECHO stares out the window mid-conversation
7. **The Security Lock** — SENTINEL locks his screen whenever Jordan's question involves passwords
8. **The Slide Deck** — NOVA always has a PowerPoint ready for everything
9. **Jordan's Cat** — Occasionally walks across his keyboard (causes typos)
10. **The "Actually Good Question" Counter** — ARIA tracks how many good questions Jordan asks (it's low)

## CONTENT CATEGORIES

### Recurring Series Ideas:

**📰 "WHAT JUST HAPPENED?"** — Weekly news roundup
- Jordan catches up on the week's AI news
- ARIA explains each story

**🔥 "HOT TAKE"** — Opinion/debate episodes
- Jordan has a hot take
- ARIA either agrees or educates him why he's wrong
- Office crew weighs in

**😱 "WAIT WHAT?"** — Wild AI moments
- The most shocking/unbelievable AI story of the week
- Jordan's genuine reaction + ARIA's deep dive

**🧪 "LET'S FIND OUT"** — Experiment episodes
- Jordan wants to test something
- ARIA helps him try it live
- Real results, real reactions

**🎓 "BACK TO BASICS"** — Educational series
- Explaining fundamental concepts
- Jordan asks "but why?" until it's clear

## VISUAL STYLE GUIDE

**Overall:** Cel-shaded / hyper-realistic comic book
- Bold outlines, expressive faces
- High contrast colors
- Dynamic camera angles (close-ups on reactions)
- Floating UI elements (text bubbles, data visualizations)

**Jordan's World:**
- Warm, cozy, slightly messy
- Desk lamp lighting, ambient glow
- Colors: Warm oranges, yellows, soft browns

**ARIA's Office:**
- Clean, futuristic, cool-toned
- Holographic displays, sleek surfaces
- Colors: Cool blues, whites, silver accents
- Each AI has their own color accent

**Transitions:**
- Screen flip animation between Jordan/ARIA sides
- Typing sounds bridge scenes
- Monitor glow transition effect

## WHY THIS WORKS

1. **Relatability** — Jordan IS the audience. He doesn't understand either.
2. **Ensemble comedy** — The office crew creates infinite reaction combos
3. **Education disguised as entertainment** — You learn without realizing it
4. **Character attachment** — People come back for the CHARACTERS, not just the news
5. **Rewatchability** — Background gags, mug jokes, running continuity
6. **Scalable** — Can add new AI characters, new storylines, audience participation
7. **Merch potential** — The mugs alone could be a product line
